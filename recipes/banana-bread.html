Banana Bread

Home

Ingredients

  • All-purpose Flour: 2 cups
  • Baking Soda: 1 teaspoon
  • Salt: 1/4 of a teaspoon
  • Brown Sugar: 3/4 of a cup
  • Butter: Half a cup
  • Eggs: 2, beaten
  • Bananas: 2 to 3 cups of mashed, overripe bananas

Why I Love Banana Bread

There's something about the heft of a freshly made, warm banana bread that lets you know in advance that you're going to eat far too much at once. It's rare in our household for a fresh loaf to make it through two full days before being completely consumed. Furthermore, there's a great degree of versatility in what you might assume to be a very simple sweet treat. It goes well on its own, with butter or preserves, or even with custard like a bread and butter pudding. You just can't beat the homely, comfortable vibe that's provided by a freshly made loaf of warm banana bread, paired with a cup of tea made by someone you love. When the rain outside looks to rattle even Noah in his arc, and the wind comes howling through those gaps in the door that you were meant to deal with, banana bread provides the comfort you need it make it through the harsh night. Not to mention baking anything simple but tasty is a great level of rewarding in its own right!

Storage

There's nothing worse than taking the time to make something from scratch, just for it to go stale two days later. Luckily, I have all the handy storage tips you could need to keep the last bite tasting just as good as the first! Most importantly, make sure to allow the bread to cool fully before preparing it for freezing to avoid causing your freezer to sweat! Then make sure to carefully wrap it in a layer of baking paper followed by a few layers of foil. This will prevent the bread from getting freezer burnt during storage. Storing it this way should allow it to keep for up to three whole months. Another helpful tip is to cut the bread up before freezing so that you can unfreeze portions as you need them, whether it be for lunch or just for a treat!

Instructions

  1. Before we get started, preheat your oven to 350 degrees Farenheit or 175 degrees Celcius and grease your 9x5 loaf pan
  2. Next take your dry ingredients, flour, baking soda, and salt in a large bowl. In a separate bowl, beat your brown sugar and butter together either by hand or with an electric whisk until smooth.
  3. Now take your brown sugar and butter mixture and stir in the eggs and mashed bananas until they're well mixed. Combine this with your flour mixture that you made earlier with the dry ingredients.
  4. Pour your newly formed batter into the loaf pan and place it into the oven until a knife or toothpick emerges from the centre completely clean. This takes roughly 60 mins.
  5. Don't try to remove the bread from the pan immediately, instead leave it to cool for roughly ten minutes to make it much easier to remove. Then place on a wire rack to cool completely.
  6. That's it! You're done! Enjoy your freshly made banana loaf and remember, if you can't finish it all now, make sure to freeze some for later!
